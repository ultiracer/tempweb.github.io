**CONSULT WITH A QUALIFIED HEALTHCARE PROFESSIONAL BEFORE BEGINNING ANY EXERCISE PROGRAM.**

- Have a history of **heart disease, high blood pressure, or other cardiovascular conditions**

- Have a history of **injuries** (musculoskeletal, joint, or soft tissue)
- Are over age **45 (men)** or **55 (women)**
- Have **obesity** (BMI > 30) or are significantly overweight

- Have **neurological conditions** (epilepsy, seizure disorders, etc.)
- Take **medications** that may affect heart rate or exercise capacity
- Have **any other medical condition** that could be affected by physical activity
- Have been **sedentary** for an extended period

### 6.3 Stop If Symptoms Occur

**⚠️ EMERGENCY WARNING SIGNS ⚠️**

**STOP EXERCISING IMMEDIATELY IF YOU EXPERIENCE:**

- **Chest pain, tightness, or discomfort** (especially radiating to arms, neck, or jaw)
- **Unusual or excessive shortness of breath**
- **Dizziness, lightheadedness, or feeling faint**
- **Irregular or rapid heartbeat** (palpitations)
- **Severe muscle or joint pain**
- **Nausea or vomiting**
- **Severe fatigue or weakness**
- **Vision changes or blurred vision**
- **Severe headache**
- **Numbness or tingling** (especially in extremities)
- **Confusion or disorientation**
- **Any other unusual symptoms**

**SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION IF SYMPTOMS PERSIST OR WORSEN.**

Call emergency services (911 in the US) if you experience severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, or other life-threatening symptoms.

### 6.4 Know Your Limits

**Exercise Safely and Progressively:**

- **Start slowly** if you are new to exercise or returning after a break
- **Increase intensity gradually** over weeks or months, not days
- **Do not attempt exercise intensities beyond your current fitness level**
- **Listen to your body** and respect signs of fatigue or distress
- **Stay hydrated** before, during, and after exercise
- **Warm up** before intense exercise and **cool down** afterward
- **Get adequate rest and recovery** between training sessions
- **Follow recommended balance programs** designed by qualified professionals
- **Do not exercise** when ill, injured, or extremely fatigued
- **Maintain proper form and technique** to prevent injury

### 6.5 Proper Equipment Use

**Equipment Safety Requirements:**

- **Use equipment ONLY as instructed** in user manuals, safety guides, and on-screen instructions
- **Inspect equipment before EACH use** for:
  - Loose bolts or connections
  - Worn or damaged parts
  - Unusual noises or vibrations
  - Proper function of all components
- **Do NOT use damaged or modified equipment**
- **Do NOT exceed weight limits** specified for equipment
- **Ensure proper setup** according to manufacturer specifications
- **Maintain equipment** according to recommended maintenance schedules
- **Use equipment in a safe environment:**
  - Level, stable surface
  - Adequate clearance around equipment
  - Proper ventilation
  - Free from tripping hazards
- **Secure loose clothing and hair** to prevent entanglement
- **Wear appropriate footwear** (athletic shoes with good grip)
- **Supervise children** and keep them away from equipment
- **Do NOT use equipment while impaired** (alcohol, drugs, medications that cause drowsiness)
