## 5. Research Data Sharing

### 5.1 Purpose and Scope

**We may share anonymized data with qualified researchers for scientific and medical research purposes.**

Research areas may include:
- Exercise physiology and wellness science
- Cardiovascular health and fitness
- Wellness optimization and progress
- Biomechanics and human movement
- Public health and wellness trends
- Medical research related to exercise and health outcomes

**Potential Research Partners:**
- Universities and academic institutions
- Research hospitals and medical centers
- Government health agencies (NIH, CDC)
- Non-profit research organizations
- Institutional Review Board (IRB) approved studies

### 5.2 Data Included in Research Sharing

**The following anonymized data may be shared with researchers:**

**Exercise Sensor Data:**
- Speed and velocity measurements
- Heart rate data
- Cadence (pedaling rate)
- Power output (watts)
- Position and posture measurements
- Gyroscope and accelerometer data
- Resistance and effort levels

**Session Data:**
- Session duration
- Distance traveled
- Timestamps (date and time of sessions)
- Session frequency and patterns
- Wellness trends over time

**Limited Demographic Information:**
- Age (may be provided as age range, e.g., "25-34", for enhanced anonymization)
- Height
- Biological sex (if provided)
- Weight (initial and changes over time, if provided)

**Calculated Metrics:**
- Estimated calories burned
- Intensity zones and levels
- Heart rate variability (HRV)
- Recovery metrics
- Wellness improvements over time

### 5.3 Data EXCLUDED from Research Sharing

**The following information is NEVER shared with researchers:**

**Personally Identifiable Information (PII):**
- Full name or any portion thereof
- Email address
- Physical address (home, work, or any location)
- Phone number
- IP address
- Device identifiers (IDFA, Android ID, MAC address)
- Account ID or username
- Profile pictures or avatars
- Social media accounts
- Payment information
- Emergency contact information

**Sensitive Content:**
- Comments, messages, or notes you create
- Communications with guides or other users
- Account credentials
- Any user-generated content

**Precise Location Data:**
- GPS coordinates
- Street addresses
- Specific geographic locations

### 5.4 Anonymization Standards

**We employ rigorous anonymization techniques to protect your privacy:**

**De-identification:**
- All direct identifiers are permanently removed
- Indirect identifiers (e.g., precise birth date) are generalized (e.g., age range)
- Unique data combinations that could enable re-identification are suppressed or generalized

**Aggregation:**
- Data is aggregated across multiple users where possible
- Minimum group sizes (k-anonymity) are enforced
- Statistical disclosure control techniques are applied

**Data Minimization:**
- Only data necessary for the research purpose is shared
- Granularity is reduced where possible (e.g., daily instead of second-by-second data)

**Re-identification Risk Assessment:**
- Data is reviewed to ensure it cannot be re-identified when combined with publicly available datasets
- Statistical risk analysis is performed before sharing
- External privacy experts may be consulted for high-risk datasets

### 5.5 Research Data Use Agreements

**Researchers receiving data must:**

**Sign Data Use Agreements (DUAs) that require them to:**
- Use data only for specified research purposes
- Not attempt to re-identify individuals
- Not share data with other parties without approval
- Implement appropriate security measures
- Delete or return data when the research is complete
- Comply with research ethics standards

**Obtain Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval:**
- Human subjects research must be reviewed and approved by an IRB
- Ethical standards for research conduct must be met
- Additional safeguards for vulnerable populations

**Comply with applicable laws:**
- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) - for EU residents
- CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) - for California residents
- HIPAA (if health data is considered Protected Health Information)
- Research-specific regulations (Common Rule, etc.)

**Publish findings responsibly:**
- Research results must not enable re-identification
- Aggregate-level findings only
- Individual data points are not published

### 5.6 Opt-Out of Research Data Sharing

**You have the right to opt out of research data sharing at any time.**

**How to Opt Out:**
1. Go to your Account Settings → Privacy Settings
2. Toggle "Allow Anonymous Data Sharing for Research" to OFF
3. Your preference will be saved immediately

**Effect of Opting Out:**
- Your data will NOT be included in future research datasets
- Data already shared in anonymized form cannot be retrieved (due to anonymization, we cannot identify which records were yours)
- Opting out does NOT affect your ability to use the Ultiracer Service
- You may opt back in at any time

**Default Setting:**
- Research data sharing is OPT-IN by default (you must affirmatively consent)
- OR, if included in Terms of Service acceptance, you may opt out at any time

### 5.7 Benefits of Research Data Sharing

**Why we conduct research data sharing:**
- Advance scientific understanding of exercise, health, and human wellness
- Contribute to public health initiatives
- Help develop better wellness methods and health interventions
- Support medical research that can improve lives
- Enable evidence-based fitness and health recommendations

**You are not compensated for data used in research**, but you contribute to scientific advancement that benefits society.

## 9. Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

**Technical Safeguards:**
- **Encryption in transit:** All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.3
- **Encryption at rest:** Sensitive data is encrypted in our databases using AES-256
- **Secure authentication:** Passwords are hashed using bcrypt with salt
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA):** Available for account protection
- **Regular security audits:** Penetration testing and vulnerability assessments

**Organizational Safeguards:**
- **Access controls:** Employees have access only to data necessary for their role
- **Background checks:** Employees with data access undergo background checks
- **Security training:** Regular privacy and security training for all staff
- **Incident response plan:** Procedures for detecting, responding to, and reporting breaches
- **Third-party security assessments:** Vendors are vetted for security practices

**Physical Safeguards:**
- **Secure data centers:** Industry-certified facilities with physical access controls
- **Redundancy and backups:** Data is backed up regularly to prevent loss

**Limitations:**
- No security system is perfect. We cannot guarantee absolute security.
- You are responsible for maintaining the security of your account credentials.
- Report suspected security issues to security@rdt.ai

**Data Breach Notification:**
- In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)
- We will notify relevant authorities as required by law
- We will provide information about the breach and steps to protect yourself
